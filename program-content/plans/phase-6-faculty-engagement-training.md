# Phase 6 — Faculty Engagement, Training & Minimum Requirements

*Status: Long-term (kick-off decision: standardized practices first, faculty engagement after). Elements can start opportunistically earlier.*
*Research basis: background-research.md §3, §11, §12.*

## Objective

Close the loop: stop inaccessible content at the source through faculty training, born-accessible authoring support, and — eventually — a minimum-requirements policy, so the remediation service works down a shrinking backlog instead of an endless one.

## Rationale

- Amanda's kick-off point, confirmed by research: helping faculty avoid creating inaccessible content beats remediating it forever. DOJ's own commentary says proactive beats reactive on cost and effectiveness.
- UW's three-element finding: support and central services alone don't get compliance without the third leg — **minimum faculty requirements** — but requirements only work paired with the support bargain ("meet the minimums and we'll help with everything else").
- New-content born-accessible obligations are arguably already in effect (April 2026); faculty-facing guidance on *new* content shouldn't wait for this phase's full rollout.

## Workstreams

### 6.1 Training program (assemble, don't author)
- Reuse: CCC Accessibility Center micro-courses + WebAIM Document Training (access resolved in Phase 1), Cidi Labs UDOIT guides/video library, Clemson public guides.
- Format model: UW-Whitewater's "Accessibility in Action" — short workshop series (Accessibility 101 / Slides & Video / Canvas Pages + UDOIT / Accessible Documents) + self-paced Canvas course + recordings on demand + 1:1 consults.
- Canvas Studio upload workflow as a standing training module (Phase 3 policy).

### 6.2 Born-accessible authoring support
- Accessible syllabus/course templates (DesignPLUS-style if available); default new-course shells that start accessible.
- "Use Canvas pages, not PDFs, where possible" guidance (CSUN/Highline pattern).
- UDOIT enabled + surfaced in every course with plain-language self-help.

### 6.3 Minimum-requirements policy (the long game)
- Model: UW's proposal — minimum course score threshold (e.g., UDOIT score ≥ target, analogous to UW's Ally ≥80%) paired with institutional support and indemnity.
- Requires: Senate/administration engagement, CBA review (Phase 2 groundwork), reliable scoring at scale (Phase 2), and a service that can credibly deliver the support side (Phases 1–3 mature).
- Interim posture: Cal State LA's framing — faculty aren't required to fix everything immediately; prioritize what matters.

### 6.4 Liaison network
- MSU/UW pattern: named contact per college; department-level dashboard views (Phase 5) give liaisons their own data.
- Grow from the faculty who engaged voluntarily in Phases 1–3 (CSUF's opt-in stream becomes the recruiting pool).

## Deliverables

- [ ] Training catalog live (assembled from reused materials) with completion tracking in the dashboard
- [ ] Accessible course/document templates published
- [ ] New-content guidance ("born-accessible now") communicated campus-wide
- [ ] Minimum-requirements proposal drafted with governance path mapped
- [ ] Liaison pilot in 2–3 colleges

## Metrics

Faculty training completions · % new courses meeting score threshold at creation · new-content issue rate trending down in scans · remediation backlog trending down · liaison coverage by college.
