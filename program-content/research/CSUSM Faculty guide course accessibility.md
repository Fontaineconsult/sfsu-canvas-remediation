Guide to Making Your Canvas Course Accessible
For CSUSM Faculty
🔍 Step 1: Clean Up Files Using TidyUp
🔧 How to Enable TidyUp in Your Canvas Course
In your Canvas course, click “Settings” (bottom of the left-hand navigation menu).
At the top of the Settings page, select the “Navigation” tab.
In the list of hidden tools (bottom section), find “TidyUp.”
Click the three dots (⋮) to the right of “TidyUp” and choose “Enable.”
Scroll down and click “Save.”
“TidyUp” will now appear in your course’s left-hand navigation menu.
Before making accessibility fixes, start by removing files you no longer use.

Launch TidyUp: Use the TidyUp tool in your course.
Review File Usage: TidyUp shows which files are not linked anywhere in the course.
Remove or Archive: Delete unused files or download and archive them elsewhere (e.g., OneDrive).
➡️ This streamlines your Ally report and helps you focus only on relevant content.

📊 Step 2: Review the Ally Course Accessibility Report
Open the Report: Go to Ally Course Accessibility Report in the left-hand course navigation.
Understand the Score: The top shows your overall Course Accessibility Score.
Overview Section:
View a colorful content wheel that breaks down content types.
Click “View” to go to the Content tab and see a sortable list of items with issues.
Start Fixing Accessibility Issues:
Content with the easiest issues to fix (Start button)
Fix low-scoring content (Start button)
Remaining Issues: list sorted by Severity, Issue title, and number of items affected
🛠️ Step 3: Fix Common Accessibility Issues
Use Ally’s guidance or follow these practices:

📄 Documents (PDF, Word, PPT)
Ensure documents have proper headings, lists, alt text, and reading order.
Use Microsoft Accessibility Checker before uploading.
Convert complex PDFs to accessible HTML pages when possible.
🖼️ Images
Provide meaningful alt text (describe what’s important for understanding).
Mark purely decorative images as such in Canvas.
📝 Canvas Pages & Assignments
Use built-in heading styles instead of bolding for section titles.
Ensure all links are descriptive (avoid “click here”).
Maintain high color contrast (e.g., dark text on a light background).
Use tables for data, not for layout, and include headers.
🧪 Step 4: Use Canvas Accessibility Checker (Built-in)
Click the Accessibility Checker icon in the Canvas Rich Content Editor.
Follow the prompts to fix:
Color contrast
Table headers
Image alt text
Heading structure
✅ Step 5: Check Multimedia
Captions: All videos must be captioned. Request CSUSM captioning services if needed.
Audio: Provide transcripts or descriptions.
Use Panopto or Kaltura which integrate with Canvas and offer auto-captioning.
💡 Extra Recommendations
🔁 Schedule a Monthly Accessibility Review: Use Ally and Canvas tools on a regular basis.
📚 Refer to CSU QLT Guidelines: Focus on Section 8.0 for accessibility.
👥 Collaborate with IDS or ATI: for support with remediation.
